# Product Context

## User problem

Image-generation interfaces expose different vocabulary and controls. Users need a practical way to organize subject, style, composition, lighting, camera, negative terms, and provider parameters without treating any example as a guaranteed recipe.

## Product response

- A client-side visual prompt builder with copy and local state.
- Factual use-case worksheets that encourage changing one variable and recording the interface, settings, prompt, and output.
- An image-to-prompt workflow with explicit upload disclosure and server-side validation.
- A small verified-guide surface backed by a quarantine registry rather than presenting a large unreviewed archive as authoritative.
- Clear privacy, cookies, terms, contact, about, and content-standard pages.

## Trust principles

- Say what was and was not tested.
- Do not infer expertise, popularity, commercial results, or provider performance from generated copy.
- Keep analytics optional and reversible.
- Keep unverified material out of publisher discovery.
- Treat provider features, versions, pricing, and policies as time-sensitive external facts.
