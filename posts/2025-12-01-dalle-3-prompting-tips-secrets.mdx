## Composition and Framing: Directing the AI's Camera

Remember what I said about being the art director? Don't let the AI decide where to put the camera. You're in charge! Use these phrases to control the composition.

*   **Shot Type:** Use terms like `Extreme close-up`, `Close-up`, `Medium shot`, `Full-body shot`, and `Wide shot` to tell the AI how close to get to the subject.
*   **Camera Angle:** Specify the perspective. A `Low-angle shot` makes the subject look powerful and imposing. A `High-angle shot` can make them seem small or vulnerable. Other great options include `Bird's-eye view` and `Eye-level shot`.
*   **Lighting:** You get to be a virtual gaffer! Instead of just saying "light," describe it.
    *   `Dramatic backlighting`
    *   `Soft, diffuse window light`
    *   `Hard, direct sunlight creating strong shadows`
    *   `Sinister uplighting` (so fun for spooky scenes)
    *   `Volumetric lighting` (this gives you those visible beams of light, great for dusty rooms or misty forests)

Let's put it all together:
```
A low-angle, full-body shot of a confident female knight in ornate, polished steel armor. She stands on a rocky cliff edge at sunrise, with dramatic backlighting from the rising sun creating a golden rim light around her silhouette.
```

## Common Pitfalls and How to Dodge Them

Even with the best prompts, things can go sideways. Here are a few common traps I've fallen into and how to get out of them.

### 1. The Ambiguity Trap

*   **Problem:** Your prompt is just too vague. "A fantasy warrior" could be a Viking, a Samurai, or a dozen other things.
*   **Solution:** Be hyper-specific. What kind of warrior? What are they wearing? What's their expression? What weapon are they holding? The more details you provide, the less DALL-E 3 has to fill in the blanks.

### 2. The Contradiction Conundrum

*   **Problem:** Your prompt contains conflicting ideas, like "a photorealistic cartoon character" or "a minimalist scene filled with intricate details." The AI just gets confused.
*   **Solution:** Make sure your concepts are cohesive. If you want a character to have cartoon proportions but look realistic, you could try something like: "A 3D render of a character with exaggerated, stylized features but with hyper-realistic skin textures and lighting."

### 3. The Overload Obstacle

*   **Problem:** You've listed 20 different things you want in the scene. The AI tries its best but inevitably forgets a few key elements. (I am so guilty of this).
*   **Solution:** Prioritize. Focus on the 3-5 most important elements in your scene and describe them really well. You can always iterate and add more details in a follow-up prompt.

### 4. Ignoring the "Helper"

*   **Problem:** You type a short prompt, get a fantastic image, but have no idea why it worked so well.
*   **Solution:** This is a game-changer. When you use DALL-E 3 inside ChatGPT, the model often *rewrites and expands* your prompt behind the scenes before sending it to the image generator. You can actually see this rewritten prompt! Just click on the generated image, and it will often show you the exact, more detailed prompt it used. This is one of the best ways to learn. I study these expanded prompts all the time to see how the AI fleshes out ideas.
